💡 How exceptional bases work

An exceptional base rolls one extra rune socket compared to a normal base — more runes = a stronger item, which is why the bot keeps them. The extra socket depends on the slot:
◈◈◈ 3 sockets (normal 2) — body armour, 2H maces, quarterstaves, staves, bows, crossbows
◈◈ 2 sockets (normal 1) — gloves, boots, helmets, shields, foci, 1H weapons

🧱 White bases

Exceptional bases can roll an extra rune socket (e.g. gloves with 2 instead of 1) — that's what makes them special and worth crafting on. Blank (Normal) ones are kept to craft or sell to crafters. No market listing, so your value floors don't apply; the quality and item-level gates below decide what's worth the pickup.

Setup guide
Five minutes, once. Step 3c is the one everybody misses — skip it and the bot quietly ignores everything you generate.


  

    Four questions — the same wizard a new install gets. Nothing here is destroyed by running it again.


  
What this app actually does

    
It checks what items are worth money on poe.ninja right now, and writes a pickit — a .ipd file that tells Exiled Bot 2 what to pick up. Prices move, so you press Generate again whenever you want the bot's loot list refreshed.

  


  
1 · Pick your league

    
On the ⚡ Generate tab. It auto-detects the current league — only change it if you play something else.

  


  
2 · Press ⚡ Generate

    
Pulls live prices and builds your pickit (a few seconds). The 📜 Preview tab then shows you exactly what the bot will read — nothing is hidden.

  


  
3 · Connect your bot — all three parts

    
Two settings in this app, and one line inside the bot's own config. The third is the one nobody guesses.


    
a. Settings → Exiled Bot 2 pickit folder
      The app tries to find this for you on first launch. If it didn't, hit
      Auto-detect in Settings — or Browse to it yourself, usually

      ...\ExiledBot2\Configuration\default\Pickit


    
b. Settings → Auto-copy after generate → ON
      Every Generate now lands in the bot by itself. No more copying files by hand.


    
c. ⚠ Tell the bot which file to read
      Open ...\ExiledBot2\Configuration\default\pickit.ini in Notepad and set:

      active_profile=poe2_pickit

      This must match the Output file name in Settings (which is poe2_pickit by default).


    
Why 3c matters more than anything else here: the bot only ever loads the single .ipd named by active_profile. If that name doesn't match, auto-copy still reports success, the file really does land in the folder — and the bot carries on reading whatever old pickit it was already on. Everything looks perfectly fine and nothing you generate is used. Change the output name in Settings and you must change this line too.

  


  
4 · Start the bot

    
Done. From now on: press ⚡ Generate whenever you want fresh prices, and the bot picks them up on its next run.

  


  
The two value floors (they confuse everyone)

    
Currency & items floor
      Gates currency, essences, runes, fragments, omens, gems — everything that isn't a unique. Set it high and you'll silently skip runes and essences.

    
Unique floor
      Gates unique weapons, armours, accessories, flasks, charms, jewels and relics.

    
Rare gear is not affected by either. Rare items are scored on their stats (see the 🎯 Magic & Rare tab), not priced — no floor touches them.

    Auto-floor recomputes both floors from live prices on every run ("keep the top N%") — handy at league start when nobody knows what a good floor is. Set a floor by hand and Auto-floor switches itself off, so your number sticks.

  


  
What the other tabs are for

    

      💰 Economy — everything poe.ninja prices, with per-item toggles.

      🎯 Magic & Rare — how rare gear is scored: each slot has a recipe, and a rare is kept only if its stats add up. Turn the whole set off with one switch.

      💠 Fracture — which Magic/Rare bases are worth a Fracturing Orb.

      🎲 Chance · 🛠 Craft · 💎 Exceptional — bases worth chancing, crafting on, or keeping for their sockets.

      📜 Preview — the exact file the bot reads. 🕰️ History — how your runs have changed over time.
    

  


  
If the bot seems to be ignoring good loot

    
Check these, in order:
      
1. Did you regenerate? Preview warns you when it's showing an old run.
      
2. Is 3c right? If active_profile doesn't match your output name, the bot is reading an old file.
      
3. Settings → Game data health. PoE2 renames things every patch; if a rule is hunting for a stat that no longer exists it matches nothing, silently. This checks for exactly that and names the broken rules.
      
4. Your floors. A high Currency & items floor quietly bins runes and essences.
